Ever wanted to duel another chatter?
Well you're in luck!
Simply **reply to a message** with the command `!duel` and bang it out.

Duels are a way to challenge another chatter to a shootout with their message at stake.
If the duelled person loses, their message is deleted.

Apart from deleting messages, the winner of a duel also receives a [giga-vip](/giga-vip) level.

Duels appear at the top of the screen with both player's orbs starting at the center and moving apart in preparation to shoot.

### How to play

After a duel is started, music will play and when the music ends, both parties will be able to bang it out.

To shoot, type either `!bang`, `bang bang` or `!!`.

**Be mindful of stream delay.** Typically, Twitch streams can be delayed from 1 second up to 6 seconds or more.
Try and estimate your delay and account for it to have a better chance at winning.

**If you shoot too early**, you will be on cooldown for 3 seconds.
Shooting within those 3 seconds resets the 3-second timer.
You can see if you shot early by looking at your orb on screen. If you bounces up and down, you had missed a shot.

After the duel has been won, the timer will display the time it took for the winner to fire their shot, after the music ended.

### Taking all bets

Do you think you know who's going to win the duel?
Confident in your own victory?
Put your money where your mouth is!
Once the countdown starts, anyone may place a bet on who will win.
Type `!gamba <@username> <amount>` to bet.
Amount is in [mega-vip](/giga-vip#mega-vip).
For example, `!gamba @Red_Epicness 1640` is a bet of 1.64 [gv](/giga-vip) that [Red_Epicness](https://bahms.org/usercard/red_epicness) will win the current duel.

The pot is split amongst the winners.
Each dueler has 500 [mv](/giga-vip#mega-vip) bet on thier behalf from thin air.
The more one bets, the bigger the share of the winnings they receive.

### Elo

Every duelist has an [Elo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elo_rating_system).
The higher the Elo, the quicker to the draw they likely are.
As one would expect, winning duels earns Elo, losing reduces Elo, and the amount gained or lost is determined by the difference between Elos.
Everyone starts at 1200 Elo.

The [usercard duel summary](https://bahms.org/usercard/) lists people's Elo, wins and losses.
